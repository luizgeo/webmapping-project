The Importance of Food Provision

Ecosystems provide a range of essential services, including the production of food. Land, as a fundamental resource, is used to grow crops, raise livestock, and provide habitats for wild food sources such as fungi and game. In Ireland, our diverse landscapes support a variety of agricultural practices and food production systems. However, increasing global demand for food, coupled with the pressures of climate change and land-use change, highlights the importance of understanding and managing our food-producing lands. A detailed map has been created to visualize the food production potential of different land areas. This map identifies areas with high food production capacity, such as cultivated cropland and improved pasture, as well as areas with lower food production potential, such as wild food gathering sites. By understanding these spatial patterns, we can make informed decisions to ensure sustainable food production and food security.

Understanding the Map: Vegetation Carbon Sequestration

The darkest areas on the map represent land with the highest potential for food production, such as cultivated crops and improved pastures. Lighter areas indicate land with lower food production potential, primarily used for wild food gathering.

Why It's Important:

Land provides a platform for food production, supporting both cultivated and wild food sources. Cultivated land is used to grow crops and raise livestock, while wild lands offer opportunities for foraging and hunting. As the global population grows, the demand for food increases. Understanding the food production capacity of different land areas is crucial for ensuring food security and sustainable land use. By identifying areas with high food production potential, we can prioritize these areas for agricultural production and implement strategies to protect them.

How the Map Was Created:

This map was created using data on land use, soil quality, and habitat types. Areas with high-quality soils and suitable climate conditions, such as arable land and improved pasture, are identified as having higher food production potential. The map provides a valuable tool for assessing the current and future potential of Ireland's land for food production.
